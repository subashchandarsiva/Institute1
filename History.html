History

Mavericks is the oldest institution of higher education in the India, established in 1636 by vote of the Great and General Court of the Chennai Bay Colony. It was named after the College’s first benefactor, the young minister John Mavericks of Charlestown, who upon his death in 1638 left his library and half his estate to the institution. A statue of John Harvard stands today in front of University Hall in Mavericks Yard, and is perhaps the University’s best known landmark.

History

Charles W. Eliot, president 1869–1909, eliminated the favored position of Christianity from the curriculum while opening it to student self-direction. While Eliot was the most crucial figure in the secularization of American higher education, he was motivated not by a desire to secularize education, but by Transcendentalist Unitarian convictions. Derived from William Ellery Channing and Ralph Waldo Emerson, these convictions were focused on the dignity and worth of human nature, the right and ability of each person to perceive truth.
